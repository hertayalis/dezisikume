Emily Ratajkowski and Eric Andr Are All Smiles During NYC Outing

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, August 7, 2024

Emily Ratajkowski is still enjoying her dating life.

The model was seen out and about in NYC yesterday, where she was photographed on what appeared to be a date with her rumored beau Eric André. The pair were all smiles as they walked together to lunch at Via Carota, an Italian restaurant in the West Village, per Page Six.

"They looked like they were having a nice chat," an eyewitness told the outlet. "They were both laughing and smiling."

For the outing, the My Body author layered an oversized leather jacket over a cropped tube top printed with orange, brown, and white stripes. She finished the look with baggy black pants, black Converse sneakers, a gold necklace, and black sunglasses, while wearing her hair up in a clip.

MEGA//Getty Images

Meanwhile, the stand-up comedian looked comfortable in blue-green sweatshirt, navy pants, yellow and green sneakers, and red-framed sunglasses.

The duo also reportedly went on a dinner date last Wednesday, with a source telling Page Six that they arrived in the same car for a New York Fashion Week dinner for La Ligne at Fouquet’s in downtown Manhattan, though they left the car separately to avoid paparazzi.

The rumored couple were first linked together last month, when Page Six published photos of the pair kissing on the beach while vacationing in the Cayman Islands. An additional video showed the two cuddling, with Ratajkowski wrapping her arms around André's neck as they rub noses.

The model recently opened up about dating in the public eye during an episode of her podcast High Low with EmRata, saying that it has felt "pretty weird to be watched."

"I've gone on dates where there's been no pictures, and I'm like, 'All good,' and then there's been times where literally the first time that I've met someone, we spent two hours together, and there's pictures on the Internet," she said.

"[My dates] know exactly where I'm at even with things that I don't necessarily want to share, and they don't necessarily want to know," she continued. "So, that's been a whole other aspect of just trying to keep things casual and also be cognizant of people's feelings, and that's been kind of tricky to navigate."
