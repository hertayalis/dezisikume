Who Was Chad Shea, Clay Collard Brother? Wikipedia And Age

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, July 17, 2024

Clay Collard fights with a renewed sense of purpose, honoring the memory of Clay Collard brother, Chad Shea.

Clay Collard, a powerhouse in both mixed martial arts and boxing, fights with more than just titles in mind; he fights for his late younger brother, Chad Shea.

Once a constant presence cheering from the sidelines or at home, Chad’s tragic loss in a car accident became the emotional fuel that drives Clay in every match.

Now, every time Clay steps into the ring, each punch thrown and dodge made carries the weight of a brother’s memory and love.

As he continues to claim victories and championships, it’s clear that Clay’s bouts are about more than just sport; they’re a living tribute to Chad.

Who Was Chad Shea, Clay Collard Brother?

Chad Shea was more than just a name. He was the younger brother of Clay Collard, a well-known figure in the world of mixed martial arts and boxing.

The two brothers were incredibly close. Chad always supported Clay in his fights.

He would be there, cheering him on, from the ringside or at home. Then tragedy struck. Chad Shea lost his life in a car accident. This event shook Clay to his core.

For Clay, each fight he takes on now has a deeper meaning. It’s not just about winning or losing every time he enters the ring.

It’s about honoring Chad. He fights to keep the memory of Clay Collard brother alive.

Moreover, Clay fights to live a life that he believes Chad would have wanted for him. So, it’s also for Chad whenever Clay throws a punch or dodges an attack.

Clay Collard Wikipedia And Age

Clay Collard was born on March 10, 1993. This makes him 30 years old as of today. 

As a child, he started wrestling at the age of six. Likewise, he picked up boxing at the age of 11. He attended Emery High School. There, he also shined in wrestling.

After school, Clay began his professional career. At first, he fought in local events in Utah. Quickly, he built a strong record.

Then, he got to fight in the UFC, a top fighting organization. He fought against several big names.

Some matches he won, some he lost. Eventually, he moved to the Professional Fighters League (PFL). Here, too, he showed his skills.

He has won many matches and even made it to the semi-finals of tournaments.

Similarly, Clay has a side career in boxing. He started boxing to make extra money but soon realized he was good at it.

Clay Collard Drive and Determination

The loss of a family member is hard. For Clay, losing his younger brother, Chad Shea, could have been a reason to quit. But he didn’t.

Instead, he used it as motivation. Clay fights not just for titles or for the thrill of the sport.

He fights for Chad. Every victory, every defeat carries the weight of Clay Collard’s brother’s memory.

Now, let’s talk about Clay’s recent fights. In 2023, he has been on a roll. First, he fought against Yamato Nishikawa.

He won that match easily. Next, he took on Stevie Ray. He also won that fight. And soon, he will be fighting in the semi-finals.

He is expected to face Shane Burgos. Many people are looking forward to this match.

Clay has also won several championships. He has been the Showdown Fights Lightweight Champion. Additionally, he won the SteelFist Fight Night titles in both the lightweight and welterweight divisions.
