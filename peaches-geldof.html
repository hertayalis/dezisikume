Death of Peaches Geldof

Peaches Geldof tragically died on April 7, 2014, at the age of 25, leaving her fans heartbroken. Her body was discovered at her home in Wrotham, Kent. The officers discovered and seized drug paraphernalia at the residence. Similarly, her death left her family in a state of profound sorrow and grief.

Her funeral was held at St Mary Magdalene and St Lawrence Church in Davington, Kent, on April 21, 2014. It’s the same church where she got married, where her parents got married, and where her mother was laid to rest. Geldof’s father, husband, other family members and friends, as well as representatives from the UK entertainment and fashion industries, attended her funeral.

Peaches Geldof’s body was discovered at her home on Monday, April 7, 2014, around 13:30. This was the date given in the initial media reports as the date of her death. However, evidence presented at the inquest suggested that she died after 20:00 on Sunday, April 6, 2014.

In a statement, her father, Bob Geldof, said:

“We are beyond pain. She was the wildest, funniest, cleverest, wittiest and the most bonkers of all of us. We loved her and will cherish her forever.”

In addition, her husband, now a widower, Thomas Cohen, stated in another statement:

“My beloved wife Peaches was adored by myself and her two sons Astala and Phaedra and I shall bring them up with their mother in their hearts every day.”

Inquest

On May 1, 2014, the inquest into Geldof’s death began at Gravesend Old Town Hall. On July 23, the coroner determined that the cause of her death was opioid intoxication. As a result, her death was classified as a drug-related death. There was no evidence that it was done on purpose. Peaches Geldof had been on methadone maintenance for two and a half years before her death.

In February 2014, she resumed heroin use, and 6.9 grams of the drug were discovered in her home. Furthermore, on July 3, 2015, Kent Police announced the conclusion of their investigation into her death, stating that they had “exhausted all lines of inquiry” in attempting to determine who supplied her with this Class A drug.

Education of Peaches Geldof
